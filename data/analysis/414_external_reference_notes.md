Borrowed:

- AI-supported documentation outputs require review, checking, local controls,
  and lawful data handling before downstream use.
- IM1 pairing can review whole-product documentation such as DCB0129 material,
  DPIA, medical-device registration evidence, and RFC posture, while AI-specific
  technical assurance remains a local deploying-organisation responsibility.
- DTAC and clinical-safety posture support traceable evidence retention,
  product accountability, data protection, technical security, usability, and
  clinical safety evidence.

Rejected:

- No external source was used to infer training eligibility from model confidence,
  analytics events, or local UI acceptance.
- No external source was used to store raw prompts, transcripts, or clinician
  free text in routine logs.
- No external source was treated as permission to bypass the local blueprint's
  final-human-artifact and authoritative-settlement requirements.
