Borrowed:

- Practitioner review and accountability remain explicit before AI-supported
  output influences records, communications, or downstream actions.
- Local deployment controls, safety documentation, and technical assurance remain
  part of the deploying organisation's governance posture.
- DTAC and clinical-safety expectations support traceability, auditability,
  usable controls, and safety-case evidence around AI-assisted workflow changes.

Rejected:

- No external reference was used to weaken the local requirement that assistive
  output is review-only until a final human artifact settles.
- No source was treated as permission to infer trainability from analytics logs,
  local UI acceptance, or model confidence.
- No source was used to store raw override free text in routine telemetry.
