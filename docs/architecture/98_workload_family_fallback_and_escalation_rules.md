# 98 Workload Family Fallback And Escalation Rules

## Backend Family Rules

- `wf_command_orchestration` may halt only when the profile allows command-level consequence.
- `wf_projection_read_models` may publish stale or carry-forward posture without widening command authority.
- `wf_integration_dispatch` may queue or halt independently from patient, staff, and governance shells.
- `wf_assurance_security_control` may degrade trust and freeze recovery clearance without forcing a generic outage.

## Audience Rules

- Patient surfaces preserve calm context, safe placeholder truth, and one safe next action.
- Staff surfaces preserve the current anchor, explain the bounded dependency fault, and refuse hidden writes.
- Support surfaces preserve guided repair posture and never flatten delivery ambiguity into success.
- Operations and governance surfaces keep diagnostics visible but controls frozen when the profile says so.
- Pharmacy and hub surfaces reuse the same bounded queue or placeholder law; they do not get bespoke hidden escalation.

## Recovery Rules

- Transport recovery alone never clears degraded posture.
- Publication parity, route exposure, trust freeze, and assurance hard-block state must all allow recovery before the engine emits `clear`.
- Recovery remains same-shell and tuple-bound.

## Anti-Patterns Rejected

- One degraded adapter causing a platform-wide kill switch.
- Cached capability keeping a patient CTA live after dependency authority is no longer exact.
- Projection drift being treated as current truth because the shell still has continuity.
- Queue-only or callback ambiguity being presented as successful delivery.
