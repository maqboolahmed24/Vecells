## Release Constraints

- Staff-side and internal-only surfaces may be prepared; patient-visible AI output is not open.
- Shadow capture and protected evaluation may start; visible rollout ladders remain blocked.
- Drafts and suggestions may be generated as review artifacts only; no generated output may commit final records or workflow state.
- Release-candidate and regulatory law may be frozen; no live IM1 RFC, DTAC, DCB, DPIA, or medical-device completion claim is made by this gate.
- Every future visible action must bind to route intent, command action, authoritative settlement, UI telemetry disclosure, and recovery disposition.

## Current Official Reference Interpretation

Official references support the gate's conservative posture:

- NHS England ambient scribing guidance keeps review, approval, intended-use, governance, monitoring, and training in scope.
- NHS England information-governance guidance keeps informing people, objection handling, approved-product use, and practitioner responsibility in scope.
- IM1 guidance requires RFC and updated SCAL for AI or significant functional enhancements and keeps AI-specific technical assurance with the deploying organisation.
- DTAC remains a five-area assurance framework and complements rather than replaces other approvals.
